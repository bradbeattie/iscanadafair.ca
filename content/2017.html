2017: Breaking the Promise

On January 10, 2017, then Minister of Democratic Institutions Maryam Monsef was replaced by Karina Gould. The year prior, she had spoken passionately on the subject of universal suffrage:

It is almost incomprehensible that we could ever exclude a full 50% of society from the franchise, that we could exclude indigenous peoples, ethnocultural minority groups, and those who dared to express different beliefs from those of the government of the day. … Electoral reform is the next step in this evolution toward a more inclusive system.

Karina Gould, June 2nd, 2016

Contrast the 2016 speech with Gould's interview on CBC's Metro Morning. When asked Why is it important that people at the very least believe every vote counts?, Gould had the following to say:

Because they do. … We literally count them: 1, 2, 3, 4, up to the majority that wins.

Karina Gould, January 12th, 2017

This contrast in sincerity signaled the coming change that was ultimately made public Febuary 1st.
